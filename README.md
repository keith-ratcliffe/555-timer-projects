# 555 Timer Integrated Circuit (IC) Projects

## Simple projects for learning about electronics, all centered around the popular 555 timer chip

### History of the 555 Timer IC

The 555 timer chip was invented by Swiss engineer Hans Camenzind in 1971. He designed the iconic
integrated circuit while working as an independent consultant for Signetics (later acquired by Philips).
The chip was released in 1972 and became one of the most successful and widely used ICs in history. 

Camenzind designed the circuit by hand, including the layout, which was created on Rubylith
film, a common practice before computer-aided design was possible. It was designed as a versatile,
low-cost "time machine" capable of creating precise time delays or oscillations.

### Why "555"?

The name comes from the three 5-ohm resistors used inside the chip to set voltage thresholds. Due to
its simple, reliable design (eight pins), it replaced numerous components and is still in use today
